Government Response

How Indian agencies manage disasters

Flood Disaster Management

Multiple organizations work together to manage flood disasters in India.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole in Flood Management
NDMA (National Disaster Management Authority)Sets national policies and plans for flood management, led by the Prime Minister.
NDRF (National Disaster Response Force)Acts as the specialized rescue team, using boats and gear for on-the-ground rescue operations.
CWC (Central Water Commission)Responsible for tracking river levels and providing flood forecasts for major rivers.
IMD (India Meteorological Department)Issues heavy rainfall warnings and weather alerts that can lead to floods.
ISRO/NRSCProvides crucial satellite imagery and maps of flood-affected regions to aid rescue efforts.

Earthquake Disaster Management

Several key bodies are involved in mitigating earthquake risks and managing the response.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole in Earthquake Management
NDMA (National Disaster Management Authority)Issues official life-saving guidelines for safety before, during, and after an earthquake.
IMD (India Meteorological Department)Operates seismographs to measure the magnitude and intensity of earthquakes across the country.
BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards)Divides India into seismic zones and sets the mandatory safety codes for constructing earthquake-resistant buildings.
NDRF / SDRFCarries out search and rescue operations in collapsed structures and provides immediate relief to victims.

Forest Fire Management

A combination of central and state agencies work to prevent and control forest fires.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole in Forest Fire Management
FSI (Forest Survey of India)Uses satellite mapping to identify active fires and publishes annual fire risk reports.
State Forest DepartmentsManages on-ground firefighting teams, builds fire lines, and conducts controlled burns to reduce fuel load.
NDMADevelops the national guidelines for fire response and provides funding to states for firefighting equipment.
NGOs & Wildlife TrustsSupport local communities with training and provide essential firefighting equipment.

Landslide Disaster Management

Specialized scientific and response agencies collaborate to manage landslide risks.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole in Landslide Management
GSI (Geological Survey of India)Acts as the lead scientific body responsible for conducting landslide hazard mapping and zonation.
NDMACoordinates national policy and guidelines for landslide management.
NDRF / SDRFCarries out the critical on-site search and rescue operations after a landslide occurs.
MoRTH (Road Transport & Highways)Manages slope stabilization projects along highways in landslide-prone areas to protect transport routes.
NIDM (National Institute of Disaster Management)Focuses on training and capacity building for professionals and communities in landslide safety.
