Landslide Mitigation Course

Understanding, preventing, and responding to landslides

Module 1: Introduction to Landslides

A landslide is the movement of rock, debris, or earth down a slope, common in Indiaâ€™s Himalayan and Western Ghats regions. They are caused by heavy rain, earthquakes, deforestation, and unscientific construction.

Types and Effects

Types range from rapid rockfalls and debris flows to very slow 'creep'. Effects include loss of life and property, river blockages that risk flash floods, and major disruption to transport routes.

Module 2: Prevention & Safety

Safety Measures
Before a Landslide

Learn if your area is high-risk, plan evacuation routes, and watch for warning signs like new cracks in the ground, tilted poles, or odd rumbling sounds.

During a Landslide

Evacuate immediately. Move away from the slide path toward higher ground.If trapped, curl into a ball and protect your head.

After a Landslide

Stay clear of the slide zone as secondary slides can follow. Report injuries and wait for authorities to declare the area safe before returning.

Prevention & Control Methods
Engineering Strategies

Includes building retaining walls, using rock netting on cliffs, and improving drainage systems to keep slopes stable.

Planning Strategies

Involves creating hazard zonation maps to guide construction, afforestation (planting trees) to bind soil, and developing early warning systems.

Module 3: Government Response

Specialized scientific and response agencies collaborate to manage landslide risks.

Key Agencies & Roles

Module 4: Aftermath - Disaster Response

The immediate area of a landslide remains extremely dangerous.

If You Are Alone

Your priority is to get away from the unstable area.

  • Stay clear of the slide zone, as secondary slides and debris flows are common.
  • Check yourself for injuries and move to stable, higher ground.
  • Avoid broken utility lines and be alert for other dangers like fallen trees or rocks.
  • Do not return to the area until authorities have declared it safe.

If Others Are Injured

Do not enter the slide zone. Your best course of action is to get help from professionals.

  • Contact the NDRF/SDRF or local police immediately. Provide the exact location and number of people trapped or injured.
  • Do not attempt to dig people out yourself, as this can cause further collapses.
  • From a safe distance, try to direct rescue teams to the location of victims.
