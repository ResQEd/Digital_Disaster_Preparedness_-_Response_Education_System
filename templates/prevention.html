Flood Prevention & Safety

Prevention Strategies
Structural Methods

These are physical constructions to control floodwaters.

  • Building embankments (flood walls) and dams/reservoirs.
  • River dredging to increase water-carrying capacity.
Non-Structural Methods

These involve planning and policy to reduce risk.

  • Creating zoning laws to stop construction in risky areas.
  • Developing early warning systems and managing watersheds through afforestation.

Rescue & Relief Efforts

Flood response focuses on saving lives and providing immediate support:

  • Rescue Operations: NDRF and SDRF teams use boats to evacuate people.
  • Emergency Supplies: Food, tents, and medicine are stocked and distributed.
  • Shelters: People are moved to safe locations with clean water and food.
  • Financial Relief: Funds from the SDRF help victims recover and rebuild.

Earthquake Prevention & Safety

Safety Measures (Drop, Cover, Hold)
Before an Earthquake

Secure heavy furniture to walls, prepare an emergency kit, and identify safe spots in your home.

During the Shaking

DROP to the floor, take COVER under a sturdy table, and HOLD ON until the shaking stops. [cite_start]Do not run outside or use elevators. [cite: 225, 229]

After the Earthquake

Expect aftershocks. Check for injuries and stay away from damaged buildings or broken power lines.

Earthquake-Resistant Buildings

Modern buildings in high-risk zones follow strict codes to ensure safety. Key features include:

  • Ductility: The ability for a building to bend without breaking.
  • Shear Walls: Special reinforced concrete walls that resist sideways forces from an earthquake.
  • Reinforced Concrete: Using steel bars inside concrete to make columns and beams much stronger.

Forest Fire Prevention & Safety

Prevention and Control Methods
MethodHow It Works
Fire LinesCleared strips of land (3–5m wide) that act as a barrier to stop flames from jumping between forest blocks.
Awareness ProgramsWorkshops for villagers and briefings for tourists on fire risks and safety protocols.
Satellite MonitoringISRO & FSI use real-time thermal imaging to detect hotspots and small fires before they spread.
Modern TechnologyDrones with infrared cameras and water-spraying helicopters are used to reach and control fires in remote spots.

Personal Safety & Rescue
Personal Safety Tips
  • Never burn leaves, light campfires, or smoke in dry forest areas.
  • Follow local forest department alerts during the fire season.
  • When visiting forests, always know your evacuation routes.
Rescue and Response

Trained fire watchers patrol high-risk areas, and state forest departments deploy helicopters and water tankers to fight large fires.

Landslide Prevention & Safety

Safety Measures
Before a Landslide

Learn if your area is high-risk, plan evacuation routes, and watch for warning signs like new cracks in the ground, tilted poles, or odd rumbling sounds.

During a Landslide

Evacuate immediately. Move away from the slide path toward higher ground.If trapped, curl into a ball and protect your head.

After a Landslide

Stay clear of the slide zone as secondary slides can follow. Report injuries and wait for authorities to declare the area safe before returning.

Prevention & Control Methods
Engineering Strategies

Includes building retaining walls, using rock netting on cliffs, and improving drainage systems to keep slopes stable.p>

Planning Strategies

Involves creating hazard zonation maps to guide construction, afforestation (planting trees) to bind soil, and developing early warning systems.
