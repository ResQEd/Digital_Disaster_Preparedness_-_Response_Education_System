Cyclone Management Course

Learn how cyclones form, how warnings work, and how communities prepare & respond.

Module 1: Introduction to Cyclones

Cyclones (tropical cyclones, hurricanes, typhoons) are intense low-pressure systems that develop over warm ocean waters. They bring strong winds, heavy rain, storm surge and coastal flooding â€” causing major damage to life, property, agriculture, and infrastructure.

How Cyclones Form
  • Warm sea surface temperatures supply energy.
  • Low-level convergence and rising air create thunderstorms.
  • Coriolis effect provides rotation (so cyclones form away from equator).
  • Organized convection and low vertical wind shear allow strengthening.

Impacts
  • Extreme winds damaging houses, power lines and trees.
  • Storm surge causing coastal inundation and erosion.
  • Torrential rain and freshwater flooding inland.
  • Long-term economic and agricultural losses.

Notable Indian examples: the 1999 Odisha Super Cyclone and Cyclone Amphan (2020).

Module 2: Prediction & Early Warning

Modern forecasting combines satellites, buoys, radar, and numerical weather models to predict cyclone track, intensity and likely impacts. Accurate, timely warnings save lives.

Tools & Systems
  • Satellite imagery for storm structure and movement.
  • Doppler radar for coastal and nearshore precipitation/wind.
  • Numerical weather prediction models for track/intensity forecasts.
  • Buoys and tide gauges for sea-level and surge monitoring.

Public Warnings & Watches
  • Understand warning categories (e.g., watch vs warning, wind speed bands).
  • Official channels: IMD bulletins, state disaster control rooms, radio, SMS alerts and sirens.
  • Follow evacuation orders â€” do not wait until the last minute.

Module 3: Preparedness & Safety

Preparation at household and community level dramatically reduces cyclone impacts.

Before a Cyclone
  • Prepare an emergency kit (water, food, medicines, torch, batteries, documents).
  • Know local high-ground evacuation routes and designated cyclone shelters.
  • Secure loose objects, reinforce windows/doors, trim trees near buildings.
  • Keep livestock and important documents safe and accessible.

During & After a Cyclone
  • During: Stay indoors away from windows; move to an interior room or shelter.
  • Avoid coastal areas during storm surge; follow evacuation instructions strictly.
  • After: Beware of downed power lines, contaminated water, and weakened structures.
  • Report emergencies to local authorities and use official relief channels for aid.

Module 4: Government Response

Disaster response in India involves coordination between multiple agencies across national and state levels.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole
IMDPrimary meteorological agency issuing cyclone forecasts and warnings.
NDMASets national disaster policies and coordinates preparedness measures.
NDRF / SDRFConducts search, rescue and relief operations in affected regions.
State Disaster Management AuthoritiesLead local evacuation, sheltering and relief activities.
Coast Guard & NavyAssist in coastal rescues, evacuations and logistics support.

Relief & Recovery
  • Immediate relief: food, water, medical camps and temporary shelters.
  • Damage assessment for long-term recovery and rehabilitation funding.
  • Restore power, drinking water and communications rapidly to speed recovery.
