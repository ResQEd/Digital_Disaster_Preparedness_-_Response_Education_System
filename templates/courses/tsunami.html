Tsunami Management Course

Understanding, predicting, and preparing for tsunamis

Module 1: Introduction to Tsunamis

Tsunamis are giant sea waves caused mainly by undersea earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, or landslides. They can travel across oceans and strike coasts with devastating force, causing massive destruction and loss of life.

Causes of Tsunamis
  • Underwater Earthquakes (major cause)
  • Volcanic Eruptions near coastlines
  • Submarine Landslides
  • Meteorite Impacts (rare)

Effects and Impact

Tsunamis can cause:

  • Massive coastal flooding
  • Infrastructure destruction
  • Contamination of freshwater sources
  • Loss of lives, homes, and livelihoods

Example: The 2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami killed over 230,000 people across 14 countries.

Module 2: Prediction & Early Warning

Tsunami Detection Systems

Tsunami warnings are based on seismic and sea-level data:

  • Seismographs detect undersea earthquakes.
  • Tsunami buoys (DART) record sea level changes.
  • Satellite data and tide gauges confirm tsunami movement.

India‚Äôs INCOIS (Indian National Centre for Ocean Information Services) provides real-time tsunami alerts.

Warning Signs for Public
  • Strong or long earthquake felt near the coast.
  • Sudden retreat of sea water exposing the ocean floor.
  • Unusual loud ocean sounds.
  • Official alerts from authorities via sirens, radio, or SMS.

Module 3: Safety & Preparedness

Preparation and awareness are key to minimizing tsunami impact.

Before a Tsunami
  • Know your local evacuation routes and high-ground shelters.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and first aid.
  • Stay informed through INCOIS and local authorities.

During a Tsunami
  • Immediately move to higher ground after an earthquake.
  • Do not wait for official warning if you feel strong tremors.
  • Stay away from the beach ‚Äî the second wave is often stronger.
