Nuclear Disaster Management Course

Learn detection, evacuation, radiation safety, and long-term recovery.

Module 1: Introduction to Nuclear Incidents

Nuclear incidents range from small radiological releases to large-scale accidents at nuclear power plants. They pose risks from ionizing radiation, contamination, fire, and secondary hazards.

Types of Nuclear/Radiological Events
  • Accident at a nuclear power plant (core melt, release).
  • Radiological dispersal device (dirty bomb).
  • Transport/industrial radiological source release.
  • Intentional malicious release (rare but critical to plan for).

Health Effects of Radiation

Exposure consequences depend on dose and duration:

  • Low doses: increased long-term cancer risk.
  • Medium doses: nausea, vomiting, temporary drop in blood counts.
  • High doses: acute radiation syndrome (potentially fatal).

Public Warning & Communication

Alerts must be clear, timely and actionable: sirens, SMS, radio/TV, and social media. Messages should specify whether to evacuate, shelter-in-place, or take KI (potassium iodide).

Module 3: Evacuation, Sheltering & Personal Safety

Rapid decisions save lives. Understand zones, routes and safe behaviour.

Evacuation vs Shelter-in-Place
  • Evacuate when authorities order movement away from danger zones (preferably upwind and uphill).
  • Shelter-in-Place when short-term containment reduces exposure (seal windows/doors, turn off ventilation).
  • Have a pre-planned route and a backup; know local evacuation centres.

Personal Protective Actions
  • Remove contaminated clothing and seal it in a plastic bag.
  • Wash exposed skin and hair thoroughly with soap and water.

  • PPE (for responders): respirators, full suits, gloves and dosimeters.

Module 4: Medical Response & Decontamination

Triage & Treatment

Medical response prioritizes life-saving treatment, decontamination, and dose assessment.

  • Triage exposed vs contaminated vs injured patients.
  • Immediate decontamination reduces further uptake of radionuclides.
  • Long-term monitoring for radiation sickness and psychological support.

Decontamination Steps
  1. Remove outer clothing and shoes carefully.
  2. Use running water and soap to wash body and hair.
  3. Contain contaminated items and label them for safe disposal.
  4. Monitor with detectors; repeat washing if needed.

Module 6: Long-term Recovery & Environmental Remediation

Recovery focuses on safe return, cleanup, food/water safety and mental health support.

Environmental Monitoring & Cleanup
  • Ongoing sampling of soil, water and food supplies.
  • Decontamination of infrastructure and controlled disposal of radioactive waste.
  • Restrictions on agriculture/fishing until safe thresholds are met.

Social & Psychological Recovery

Provide counselling, consistent public communication and livelihood support for affected communities.
