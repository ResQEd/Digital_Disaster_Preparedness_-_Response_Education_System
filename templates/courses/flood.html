Flood Management Course

Understanding, preventing, and responding to floods

Module 1: Introduction to Floods

Floods in India are powerful, recurring phenomena mainly triggered by the South-West Monsoon. They cause the overflowing of rivers and streams, flooding land that is usually dry and leading to loss of life, disruption to livelihood, and big economic setbacks.

Module 2: Prevention & Safety

Prevention Strategies
Structural Methods

These are physical constructions to control floodwaters.

  • Building embankments (flood walls) and dams/reservoirs.
  • River dredging to increase water-carrying capacity.
Non-Structural Methods

These involve planning and policy to reduce risk.

  • Creating zoning laws to stop construction in risky areas.
  • Developing early warning systems and managing watersheds through afforestation.

Rescue & Relief Efforts

Flood response focuses on saving lives and providing immediate support:

  • Rescue Operations: NDRF and SDRF teams use boats to evacuate people.
  • Emergency Supplies: Food, tents, and medicine are stocked and distributed.
  • Shelters: People are moved to safe locations with clean water and food.
  • Financial Relief: Funds from the SDRF help victims recover and rebuild.

Module 3: Government Response

Multiple organizations work together to manage flood disasters in India.

Module 4: Aftermath - Disaster Response

Floodwaters can be dangerous and contaminated. Your first priority is to get to safety.

If You Are Alone

Focus on your immediate safety and staying informed.

  • Move to the highest ground possible and wait for rescuers.
  • Do not walk or drive through moving floodwaters. The water can be deeper and faster than it appears.
  • Avoid contact with floodwater as it may be contaminated with sewage or hide sharp objects.
  • Listen to a battery-powered radio for official evacuation orders and updates.

If Others Are Injured

Your safety comes first; do not attempt a rescue that puts you in danger.

  • Contact the NDRF, SDRF, or local emergency services with your location and the number of people affected.
  • Help others move to higher ground if it is safe to do so.
  • Do not enter fast-moving water to rescue someone unless you are a trained professional.
  • Offer reassurance and help keep victims calm and warm while waiting for help.
