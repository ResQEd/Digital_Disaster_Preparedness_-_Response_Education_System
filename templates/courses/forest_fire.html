Forest Fire Control Course

Understanding, preventing, and responding to forest fires

Module 1: Introduction to Forest Fires

A forest fire is an uncontrolled blaze spreading through vegetation, most often occurring in India's hot, dry summer months (Marchâ€“June). Causes can be natural (lightning) or human (burning waste, careless smoking).

Types of Forest Fires
  • Surface Fires: Burn along the ground through leaf litter. Common in central and southern India.
  • Crown Fires: Intense fires that jump between treetops. Occur in dense Himalayan forests.
  • Ground Fires: Slow, smoldering fires below the surface. Found in peat-rich forests of the North-East.

Prone Areas and Effects

High-risk areas include the Himalayan foothills, Central India, and the Western Ghats. Effects include loss of biodiversity, soil damage, severe air pollution, and economic losses from timber and tourism.

Personal Safety & Rescue
Personal Safety Tips
  • Never burn leaves, light campfires, or smoke in dry forest areas.
  • Follow local forest department alerts during the fire season.
  • When visiting forests, always know your evacuation routes.
Rescue and Response

Trained fire watchers patrol high-risk areas, and state forest departments deploy helicopters and water tankers to fight large fires.

Module 4: Aftermath - Disaster Response

Even after the main fire passes, the area can be hazardous.

If You Are Alone

Ensure you are completely clear of danger and let people know you are safe.

  • Move to a designated safe meeting point or a large, clear area with no vegetation.
  • Be aware of hot spots or smoldering embers that could flare up again.
  • Watch out for fire-weakened trees or branches that could fall.
  • Once you are safe, contact family or emergency services to let them know your status.

If Others Are Injured

Getting everyone out of the smoke and heat is the top priority.

  • Help guide people away from the fire towards a safe, open area.
  • If someone has minor burns, cool the area with clean water. For serious burns, call for immediate medical help.
  • For smoke inhalation, move the person to fresh air and help them stay calm.
  • Contact the forest department or fire services to report injuries and your location.
