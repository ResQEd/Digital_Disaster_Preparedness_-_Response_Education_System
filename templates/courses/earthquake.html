Earthquake Safety Course

Understanding, preparing for, and responding to earthquakes

Module 1: Introduction to Earthquakes

An earthquake is a sudden, violent shaking of the ground caused by a rapid release of energy in the Earth's crust. Nearly 59% of India's land is vulnerable to tremors due to the Indian tectonic plate pushing into the Eurasian plate.

Causes and Types

The main cause is Plate Tectonics. Human-induced causes include large dams (Reservoir-Induced Seismicity) and mining. The most common and destructive are Tectonic Earthquakes, especially shallow ones which cause more surface damage.

India's Seismic Zones

India is divided into four zones:

  • Zone V (Very High Risk): Northeast India, Kashmir, Rann of Kutch.
  • Zone IV (High Risk): Delhi, North Bihar, Indo-Gangetic Plains.
  • Zone III (Moderate Risk): Chennai, Mumbai, Kolkata.
  • Zone II (Low Risk): Parts of Deccan Plateau.

Module 2: Prevention & Safety

Safety Measures (Drop, Cover, Hold)
Before an Earthquake

Secure heavy furniture to walls, prepare an emergency kit, and identify safe spots in your home.

During the Shaking

DROP to the floor, take COVER under a sturdy table, and HOLD ON until the shaking stops. Do not run outside or use elevators.

After the Earthquake

Expect aftershocks. Check for injuries and stay away from damaged buildings or broken power lines.

Earthquake-Resistant Buildings

Modern buildings in high-risk zones follow strict codes to ensure safety. Key features include:

  • Ductility: The ability for a building to bend without breaking.
  • Shear Walls: Special reinforced concrete walls that resist sideways forces from an earthquake.
  • Reinforced Concrete: Using steel bars inside concrete to make columns and beams much stronger.

Module 3: Government Response

Several key bodies are involved in mitigating earthquake risks and managing the response.

Key Agencies & Roles
AgencyRole in Earthquake Management
NDMA (National Disaster Management Authority)Issues official life-saving guidelines for safety before, during, and after an earthquake.
IMD (India Meteorological Department)Operates seismographs to measure the magnitude and intensity of earthquakes across the country.
BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards)Divides India into seismic zones and sets the mandatory safety codes for constructing earthquake-resistant buildings.
NDRF / SDRFCarries out search and rescue operations in collapsed structures and provides immediate relief to victims.

Module 4: Aftermath - Disaster Response

The danger is not over when the shaking stops. Aftershocks are a major risk.

If You Are Alone

Carefully assess your situation and surroundings.

  • Be prepared for aftershocks.If they happen, Drop, Cover, and Hold On.
  • Check yourself for injuries first before moving.
  • If it is safe, move to an open area away from damaged buildings, walls, and power lines.
  • If you suspect a gas leak or electrical damage, turn off the mains if you can do so safely.

If Others Are Injured

Help where you can without becoming a victim yourself.

  • Check on others and provide basic first aid if you are trained and it is safe.
  • Do not move seriously injured people unless they are in immediate danger of further injury.
  • Contact emergency services and report the number of injured people and their conditions.
  • Help extinguish small fires if possible, but evacuate immediately in case of a large fire.
