Module 2: Early Detection & Surveillance Systems

Disease Surveillance Networks
  • IDSP (Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme) for nationwide monitoring.
  • Rapid Response Teams (RRTs) for immediate field investigation.
  • Laboratory testing, genome sequencing, and contact tracing tools.

Public Health Communication

Timely information sharing prevents panic and misinformation. Use official media, health portals, and verified sources for alerts and updates.

Module 3: Containment, Quarantine & Isolation

Containment strategies minimize the spread and protect communities.

Quarantine vs Isolation
  • Quarantine: Restricts movement of individuals exposed but not yet symptomatic.
  • Isolation: Separates confirmed infected individuals from healthy populations.
  • Maintain hygiene, PPE use, and infection control during both processes.

Contact Tracing & Field Response
  • Identify and monitor close contacts of infected individuals.
  • Deploy field workers equipped with digital tracking and PPE.
  • Disinfect public spaces, transportation, and shared facilities.

Module 4: Medical & Public Health Response

Healthcare Infrastructure
  • Set up isolation wards, testing labs, and ICU capacity.
  • Ensure adequate PPE, oxygen, ventilators, and medicine stockpiles.
  • Train healthcare workers in infection control and biosafety.

Vaccination & Immunization Campaigns
  • Mass immunization reduces population vulnerability.
  • Cold-chain management ensures vaccine efficacy.
  • Public awareness about vaccination importance.

Module 6: Community Awareness & Recovery

Public Education & Hygiene

  • Combat misinformation through verified sources.
  • Community volunteers aid awareness and relief efforts.

Psychosocial & Economic Recovery

Post-pandemic recovery includes mental health counselling, livelihood restoration, and strengthening healthcare systems for future resilience.
